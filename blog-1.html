Sanke Temple On The Top.

Nag Tibba, literally meaning the Serpent’s Peak, is a part of the 3 ranges of the Lesser Himalayas – Dhauladhar range, Pir Panjal range, and Nag Tibba range. Located at an elevation of 3050 m above the sea level, Nag Tibba is the highest peak in the lower Himalayas of the Garhwal region. To enjoy the clear views of Bandarpoonch peak, Kedarnath peak, and Chanabang peaks, all one has to do is take the exciting Nag Tibba trek.
